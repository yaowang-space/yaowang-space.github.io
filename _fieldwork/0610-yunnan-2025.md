    <h3>🏔️ Field Investigation at Disuga, Sanjiang Region</h3>

    In **May 2025**, I joined a field expedition to the **Sanjiang (Three Parallel Rivers) volcanic belt** in northwestern Yunnan Province, as part of our research on the **Triassic volcanic rocks** associated with the Paleo-Tethys tectonic evolution.

    The outcrop shown here is located near **Disuga**, at an elevation of over **4,600 meters**. The section exposes a sequence of **mafic to intermediate volcanic rocks** — basaltic andesites and andesitic lavas interbedded with volcaniclastic deposits — that record the arc magmatism preceding the closure of the Paleo-Tethys Ocean.

    At this altitude, I experienced **severe acute mountain sickness (AMS)** — persistent headache, nausea, and labored breathing. Despite the physical challenge, this fieldwork was one of the most memorable experiences of my graduate studies. Sampling at the roof of the Sanjiang orogen gave me a firsthand appreciation of both the geological complexity and the raw beauty of the Tibetan Plateau's southeastern margin.
